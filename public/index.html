Project Overview

The project aims to develop porous PLGA microspheres as advanced bone graft materials, optimised for handling, osteoconductivity and controlled degradation. The work bridges laboratory research with industrial manufacturing and clinical translation.

Clinical Motivation

  • Improve bone defect healing and fusion outcomes.
  • Reduce dependence on autografts and allografts.
  • Enable minimally invasive delivery of graft materials.

Research Focus

  • Design and fabrication of porous PLGA microspheres.
  • Characterisation of mechanical and degradation behaviour.
  • In vitro performance and biocompatibility studies.

Translational Pathway

  • Process scale-up and reproducibility.
  • Regulatory and quality considerations.
  • Roadmap towards pilot production and market entry.

Key Work Packages

The programme is organised into focused work packages to align laboratory tasks, industrial input and regulatory expectations.

WP1 – Material Design & Characterisation

  • PLGA selection and formulation optimisation.
  • Porosity, morphology and size distribution analysis.
  • Thermal and mechanical characterisation.

WP2 – Microsphere Fabrication

  • Process development for porous microsphere production.
  • Scale-up strategy for consistent batch quality.
  • Integration into bone graft formats or composites.

WP3 – In Vitro & Preclinical Evaluation

  • Cell viability, attachment and proliferation studies.
  • Degradation and release behaviour in vitro.
  • Preclinical model planning in line with regulations.

WP4 – Translation, QA & Market Readiness

  • Quality and documentation aligned with standards.
  • Manufacturing workflow and technology readiness.
  • Pathway from TRL 4–5 towards pilot-scale production.

Academic–Industry Collaboration

The project is supported through collaboration between academic and industrial partners, targeting real clinical and market needs.

Industry Partner
